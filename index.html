Will drinking water be a global crisis?

Water Stress Levels Per Nation

Henry Hargreaves, Alfredo Cuevas, Hei Lim Hailey Pun
hhargrea@ucsc.edu, acuevas5@ucsc.edu, hpun@ucsc.edu

Objective

To plot the water stress scores for each nation at various points in time, i.e. past, present and future. In order to calculate the water stress levels for any nation at any given time, we will do the following:

  1. Sum the amount of internal and external water for each nation, giving the total amount of available water for a given year
  2. Calculate, as a ratio, the amount of water withdrawn with respect to the total water available to a nation
  3. Find the nation which was the highest water withdrawals with respect to the amount of water they have available
  4. Divide the calculated ratio for each nation by this maximum, giving each nation a stress level score between 0 and 1.

Future water stress levels are predations, which are split into three categories: Pessimistic, Optimistic, and Business as Usual Another interesting insight we hope to provide is where the water is going in each nation, i.e. agricultural, industrial or domestic renewable freshwater resources by region.

Our main plot will be a geo json map showing the water stress levels for each nation, we will have a slider on the bottom that allows the users to see how the water stress levels change over time and what they will be in the future.
